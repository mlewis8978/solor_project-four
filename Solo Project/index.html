Quick Information

  • What do dermatologist do?
    Dermatologists are known as the “skin doctors”, they provide services to over 3000 different diseases and conditions that affect the skin, hair, and nails. Dermatologist treat 3000 different diseases and conditions They help to treat common skin issues like eczema, acne, skin infections, and even skin cancer. With such diversity in the dermatology field, it gives dermatologists lots of options to choose a certain area in which they would like to work.
  • Where do they work?
    Commonly working during the week helps to leave, nights and weekends to themselves. Many dermatologists do not work in hospitals, but they work in private practices, clinics, and many other places. On average dermatologists see 100 to 124 patients a week and spend about 9 to 12 minutes per patient.
  • How much do they make?
    Doctors choosing to work in hospitals and clinics tend to get paid around $157,000 to $325,000 a year. Those working in private practices get paid around $305,000 to $382,000 a year. In Charlotte, North Carolina, a dermatologist on average is paid $303,883 a year.

Steps to becoming a dermatologist

  • Obtain a bachelors degree
  • Pass the MCAT (Medical College Admissions Test) with a good score
  • Go to Medicial School and become a doctor
  • Intership in a general field of medicine (rememeber dermatology is not a general field) for 3 years
  • Complete another three years dermatology residency
  • Pass the American Board of Dermatology
  • You will offically be a dermatologist!
